Activity ID: A2
Activity Name: Discover security concepts used in public space.

Objective: To identify and document physical and electronic security controls deployed in public environments to deter theft and protect physical assets.

Methodology (How I did it): I surveyed public shopping spaces and captured photographs of two distinct loss-prevention security controls: Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) gates at a department store entrance, and a live public-facing CCTV monitor inside a supermarket aisle.

Results & Findings: These photographs demonstrate two core public security concepts used to combat retail shrinkage:

1. Electronic Article Surveillance (Detection): The EAS pedestals placed at the store perimeter use radio frequency (RF) or acousto-magnetic (AM) technology to detect active security tags on merchandise. They act as a hard boundary, sounding an immediate alarm if unpaid assets cross the threshold.

2. Public Display Monitors (Psychological Deterrence): The ceiling-mounted TV in the supermarket aisle intentionally broadcasts the live CCTV feed back to the public. Instead of hiding the cameras, this environmental design choice maximizes the psychological deterrent effect by making individuals explicitly aware that they are being actively watched and recorded, drastically reducing opportunistic shoplifting.
