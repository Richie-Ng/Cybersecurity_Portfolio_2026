Activity ID: A1
Activity Name: Discover security concepts used on campus.

Objective: To identify and document various physical security concepts deployed across a university campus to protect students, staff, and physical assets.

Methodology (How I did it): I observed the physical security infrastructure across the university campus. I captured two photographs documenting distinct but complementary physical security controls: a mobile security patrol vehicle and a stationary emergency call box (Help Point).

Results & Findings: These photographs illustrate a multi-layered, defense-in-depth approach to campus physical security:

1. Mobile Patrols (Deterrence & Detection): The highly visible security personnel in a marked vehicle provide active human monitoring of the campus grounds. This establishes a strong visual presence that actively discourages unauthorized or malicious activity.

2. Emergency Help Point (Communication & Response): The fixed, highly visible emergency call box features an integrated camera and intercom. It provides students and staff with an immediate, hardwired line of communication to the security operations center during a crisis, ensuring the rapid deployment of mobile patrol units to exact locations.
