Objective: To engage with the broader cybersecurity community, gain industry insights, and learn about real-world applications and historical precedents of network security.

Methodology & Findings: I attended two distinct professional and academic cybersecurity seminars to bridge theoretical knowledge with practical industry application:

2. Academic/Historical Tech Talk ("Into the Cuckoo's Egg"): Attended a deep-dive cybersecurity presentation hosted by the University Computer Club (UCC) and presented by Mark Tearle. The seminar covered the legendary 1986 digital spy-hunt conducted by Clifford Stoll at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. This provided valuable context on the foundational methodologies of digital forensics, honeypots, and intrusion detection.

Results: Successfully engaged with both modern enterprise data protection strategies and historical incident response methodologies, broadening my understanding of the cybersecurity landscape.
